India's SAHI: A Radical AI-for-Health Framework

ICTworks · Feb 24, 2026
India's Strategy for AI in Healthcare is the first LMIC health-AI strategy to launch with an operational platform (BODH for benchmarking health AI models) and explicitly prioritize "innovation over cautionary restraint." It introduces a tripartite data-gap taxonomy that distinguishes AI-critical, AI-limiting, and AI-enhancing datasets — shifting investment conversations from "we need more data" to "which data deficits block which use cases." The framework also dedicates a full pillar to outcome-oriented procurement reform, directly addressing vendor lock-in and fragmented purchasing.
💡 Key Insight

SAHI's data-gap taxonomy is directly applicable to IDinsight's health and analytics work. Instead of the usual "collect more data" recommendation, it provides a framework for advising LMIC governments on where data investment yields the highest AI return — a concrete tool for our advisory engagements.

Gender Bias in LLMs: Data Labelling and Qualitative Analysis

MERL Tech · Feb 9, 2026
Systematic tests of ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini on simulated qualitative MERL data from a women's economic empowerment project. Claude performed strongest on intersectional analysis — identifying more dimensions of identity and flagging missing data. ChatGPT showed contextual distortion, misinterpreting legitimate concerns about donor KPIs as "bias against donors." Separately, Reliabl's research found 90% of image-generation outputs for "beautiful woman" depicted light-skinned women, even when ethnicity was implied.
💡 Key Insight

For IDinsight teams using LLMs for qualitative coding or analysis: bias is subtle rather than overt, requiring extensive validation. The study confirms that prompting helps mitigate some issues but cannot fix structural biases in training data. Human oversight and model comparison (e.g., via OpenRouter) remain essential.

GABRIEL: GPT as a Measurement Tool

NBER Working Paper · Feb 2026
Shared by @Fahad Hasin
New NBER paper (Asirvatham, Mokski & Shleifer) presents GABRIEL, a software package that uses GPT to quantify attributes in qualitative data — e.g. how "pro-innovation" a speech is. Tested against 1,000+ human-annotated tasks across multiple domains, GPT was found to be generally indistinguishable from human evaluators. Results did not depend on the exact prompting strategy used, and the authors found no evidence of training data contamination.
🧪 Try it

Use GABRIEL to code qualitative survey responses or policy documents at scale for IDinsight evaluations — potentially reducing manual coding time while maintaining inter-rater reliability.

💡 Key Insight

This is the strongest evidence yet that LLMs can serve as reliable measurement instruments for qualitative data. For IDinsight's evaluation work, GABRIEL offers a concrete, tested tool for scaling qualitative coding — with the important caveat that the MERL Tech gender bias findings (see Debates) remind us that LLM-based coding still requires human validation, especially for culturally sensitive topics.
