Global Mapping of AI in Community Health Worker Programs

ICTworks ¬∑ Feb 12, 2026
A new mapping by Nate Miller identified 38 AI systems supporting community health workers across LMICs, with 87% concentrated in Sub-Saharan Africa and South Asia. Only 34% show signs of scaling beyond pilot phase ‚Äî a pattern the author calls "pilot purgatory." Over 40% of implementations are LLM-powered chatbots acting as always-on supervisors, but research shows CHWs often perceive AI as infallible, forming incorrect mental models about how these systems work.
Key Insight

Directly relevant to IDinsight's health systems portfolio. The finding that programs are launched without scaling plans, sustainable financing, or health system integration mirrors challenges we see in M&E of digital health interventions. The CHW trust gap ‚Äî treating AI outputs as infallible ‚Äî is a critical design and evaluation consideration.

Where Is Generative AI Supporting MERL Improvement in 2026?

ICTworks / MERL Tech ¬∑ Jan 20, 2026
Linda Raftree's comprehensive state-of-play for GenAI and MERL. Organizations that effectively use AI for MERL tend to be those with in-house data science capacity and clean data foundations. She identifies four archetypes of AI adoption: the Curious, the Doers, the Dreamers, and the Skeptics. More organizations are moving from talking about "potential AI applications" to sharing "actual AI applications," though many remain in the Curious stage.
Key Insight

The four archetypes framework is a useful lens for how IDinsight advises clients on AI readiness. The emphasis on data hygiene and internal capacity as prerequisites for AI-enabled MERL reinforces our own experience that AI amplifies existing data maturity rather than substituting for it.
